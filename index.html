Due to their wide applications in detection and exploration, mapping and prospecting, unmanned reconnaissance, and operations in unstructured environments, the research on resilient navigation in the wild has made significant progress and has been deployed in agriculture, forestry, animal husbandry, emergency rescue, and military, etc. However, the environment perception and motion control of unmanned platform navigation systems are affected by the unstructured, variable, and uncertain characteristics of wild environments. The reliability and stability of unmanned platform navigation systems face huge challenges. This workshop addresses the core challenges of resilient navigation in the wild, with a particular focus on traversability analysis, navigation algorithms, dataset construction, and simulation environment design in complex unstructured environments. It aims to advance the integration of cutting-edge technologies, including embodied intelligence and large-scale model, into resilient navigation, driving progress toward safety, efficiency, and interpretability.

Organizers



Bringing together leading scholars from top-tier institutions such as Tsinghua University, Peking University, and the Chinese Academy of Sciences, the workshop facilitates state-of-the-art academic presentations and interdisciplinary discussions. By promoting innovative applications of resilient navigation in the wild, it seeks to establish solid theoretical foundations and technical pathways for the development of trustworthy, interpretable, and highly generalizable resilient navigation systems, driving breakthroughs in autonomous navigation in complex real-world environments.

Workshop Content & Expected Impact

Core Topics

The primary objective of this workshop is to advance the state-of-the-art in resilient navigation in the wild by addressing critical challenges and bridging the gap between conventional structured-environment navigation and the complex demands of off-road settings. The content focuses on the latest developments in robotics and automation, including:

  • Dataset Construction for Resilient Navigation in the Wild: Demands specialized data acquisition strategies and distinctive annotation methods different from structured environments, involving task-specific elements and labels that reflect complex wild terrain and environmental factors .
  • Simulation for Resilient Navigation in the Wild: Generates dangerous, extreme, and complex scenarios hard to capture via conventional simulators or real-world data collection, providing high-quality, cost-effective support for training, testing, and deploying navigation algorithms for unstructured environments .
  • Traversability Analysis and Navigation Algorithm Design: Explores traversability analysis of heterogeneous agents in diverse unstructured environments, laying the foundation for enhancing safety, robustness, and long-range planning capabilities of navigation algorithms .

Expected Impact

To bring these cutting-edge topics to life, the workshop will feature interactive sessions, panel discussions, and live demonstrations—formats designed to foster dynamic interactions, encourage in-depth discussions, and create valuable networking opportunities. Dedicated poster sessions and roundtable discussions will serve as platforms for sharing diverse research findings and nurturing collaborative partnerships. By focusing on the unique challenges of resilient navigation in the wild, the workshop introduces fresh perspectives that complement the broader IROS program and stimulate interdisciplinary dialogue, paving the way for groundbreaking research and innovative applications .

Intended Audience

The intended audience comprises academic researchers, industry practitioners, and professionals with backgrounds in robotics, automation, and intelligent systems—especially those specializing in off-road navigation, resilient autonomous systems, and unmanned vehicle operation in complex unstructured environments. Attendees are expected to include faculty, postdoctoral researchers, graduate students, and engineers from academia and industry (focused on navigation algorithms, sensor data processing, simulation technologies) .

The workshop specifically targets members of relevant IEEE Robotics and Automation Society (RAS) Technical Committees, including:

  1. Algorithms for Planning and Control of Robot Motion
  2. Autonomous Ground Vehicles and Intelligent Transportation Systems
  3. Cognitive Robotics
  4. Computer & Robot Vision
  5. Software Engineering for Robotics and Automation
  6. Space Robotics

Based on past IROS events, approximately 50 to 80 participants are expected .

Plan to Solicit Participation


To ensure wide and diverse participation, a multi-channel outreach strategy is adopted:

  • Targeted Outreach via Established Channels: Leverage established mailing lists, professional social media platforms, and networks such as the IEEE Robotics and Automation Society to disseminate workshop details, including direct outreach to technical committees specializing in robotics, autonomous vehicles, and related areas .
  • Direct Engagement with Technical Committees: Send invitations to members of relevant technical committees (e.g., robot motion planning, cognitive robotics, computer vision) to tap into networks of established experts and emerging researchers, ensuring insights from experienced professionals and engagement of early-career researchers .
  • Utilization of Digital and Print Media: Employ targeted print media and academic bulletins (in addition to online platforms) to reach a broader audience; provide regular updates on the workshop website and social media to keep potential participants informed and engaged .

This comprehensive plan aims to attract a dynamic and diverse group, fostering interaction and collaboration between established experts and early-career researchers .
